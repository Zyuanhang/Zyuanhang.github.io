P1 Introduction¶

Since the emergence of Bitcoin in 2009, various digital currencies have begun to emerge. This type of currency, which was originally created to resist the abuse of currency and promote social fairness and efficiency, has many differences in the way of currency creation, storage, and circulation from the current global legal currency. From a social point of view. On the one hand, as an application of blockchain technology, digital currency has many good application scenarios. On the other hand, as a new thing, digital currency has many drawbacks, such as its anonymity, which is easily used by many elements for money laundering and other illegal and criminal funds and transaction.The low transparency of market information makes the price of digital currency easy to be manipulated and thus harm the interests of investors
However, from the perspective of financial market research, the development of digital currency has become a brand new type of asset. Studying this emerging financial asset, which is very different from traditional assets, can help us understand the nature of financial transactions more deeply. In this project, three different types of RNN model are used to perform regression tasks to fit the prices of Bitcoin in the past few years.

Since this is a time series data, I assume the datapoint can be predicted by previous 60 datapoints. Why 60? In stock market, 60 day MACD line is an important feature to predict the price of a stock. I tried 20 datapoints as features (MACD 20 line is also important in stock analysis.), but the results are bad comparing to 60 datapoints.

In [7]:
# For each datapoint, make the 60 datapoints right before it to be the features of it.
for i in range(60,1196):
    x_train.append(training_scaled[i-60:i, 0])
    y_train.append(training_scaled[i,0])
x_train,y_train = np.array(x_train),np.array(y_train)
x_train = np.reshape(x_train, (x_train.shape[0],x_train.shape[1],1))

P2 Model setup¶

RNN models are developed for sequence data machine learning, and has been proved to be a successful model in related applications. RNN model started from simple-RNN nodes, and then got improved with LSTM nodes, recent years witnessed the application of GRU nodes.
How would these 3 models perform in a task to fit a curve of Bitcoin prices? I have assumption that GRU is better than LSTM and LSTM is better than simple RNN. The assumption didn't come from random guess. An instiution formed from the history of RNN that the newer model beats the old one. Another reason is that I searched the internet and lots of work supported my assumption.
Is it true or not? I built 3 models to explored the answer.
